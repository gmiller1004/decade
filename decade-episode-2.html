DECADE

Episode 2: “Born to Run”

Story by Adam Carr & Greg Miller • Written by Greg Miller

TELEPLAY • v1.0

TEASER

EXT. TWO-LANE HIGHWAY - NIGHT (1974)

The Ford Torino eats blacktop under a bruised sky. Headlights cut the dark. “Born to Run” is not playing — but the rhythm of the road feels like it.

DESMOND drives, jaw set. LAYLA rides shotgun, bare feet on the dash, wind whipping her hair. The magic guitar rides in the back like a passenger that never sleeps.

DESMOND

(quiet, half to himself)
We’re really doing this. No more garage. No more “maybe someday.” Just the road and whatever the hell’s waiting at the end of it.

LAYLA

(grinning, fierce)
Then run like hell, baby. Before the town catches up.

The guitar hums low in the back seat. Desmond glances in the rearview. For half a second, golden wings flicker behind them in the glass.

He looks forward again. Says nothing.

SMASH TO BLACK.

END TEASER

ACT ONE

EXT. ROADSIDE DINER - DAWN

Greasy spoon off the highway. Desmond and Layla are the only customers. Empty plates. Coffee refills.

DESMOND

(fast, building fire)
You know what this is? This is the first real day. No more uncles telling me to get a real job. No more playing for drunks in a parking lot. We cross into California and the whole goddamn city is gonna feel it in their bones. I can feel it in my hands, Layla. The guitar — it’s like it already knows every riff I’m ever gonna need.

LAYLA

(leaning in, matching energy but with an edge)
Then don’t waste it. We’ve got two tanks of gas, thirty-seven bucks, and one axe that plays better than you ever did. That’s the hand. Play it like you mean it.

A WAITRESS drops the check. Desmond’s fingers drum the table like he’s already soloing.

DESMOND

I mean it. More than anything.

ACT TWO

EXT. TRUCK STOP PARKING LOT - LATE MORNING

Desmond plugs the guitar into a tiny battery amp he bought for ten bucks. A small crowd of truckers and travelers gathers.

He plays. Not a cover — an original, ferocious, alive. The magic is undeniable. Fingers fly. The crowd grows. Money drops into Layla’s hat.

But midway through the solo the strings suddenly feel ice-cold. Desmond’s hand stutters for a fraction of a second. In the chrome bumper of a semi, golden wings flash behind his reflection.

He finishes strong. The crowd cheers.

LAYLA

(counting cash, excited but uneasy)
Sixty-two bucks in fifteen minutes. At this rate we eat steak in Beverly Hills.

DESMOND

(grinning, but quieter)
Yeah. We will.

ACT THREE

INT. TORINO - MOVING - AFTERNOON

They’re back on the road. Layla counts the growing wad of bills. Desmond drives, eyes on the horizon.

LAYLA

We’re actually doing this. No more “someday.”

DESMOND

(fast again)
This is what I was born for. Not rotting in Des Moines. Not night classes. The road. The riff. The run. Tramps like us, baby — we were born to run.

Layla laughs, but her smile fades when she sees Desmond’s knuckles white on the wheel.

LAYLA

(softer)
You okay?

DESMOND

(beat, then the silence returns)
Yeah. Just… the guitar gets heavy sometimes.

ACT FOUR

EXT. SMALL ROADHOUSE - EVENING

Neon sign: LIVE MUSIC TONIGHT. Desmond convinces the owner to let them play one set for gas money.

Inside, a half-full bar. Desmond and Layla take the tiny stage. Keith and Taylor are gone — just the two of them now.

Desmond plugs in. The first chords hit like lightning. The crowd wakes up. He rips through a blistering set, voice raw, guitar singing. Layla sings backup, fierce and sexy.

Mid-song the supernatural price shows: Desmond’s left hand suddenly cramps hard. He pushes through, sweat pouring, but the pain is real. The crowd doesn’t notice. They roar.

ACT FIVE

INT. TORINO - LATER THAT NIGHT

They’re back on the road, money in their pockets, adrenaline high.

LAYLA

You were on fire tonight. They loved you.

DESMOND

(quiet, rubbing his hand)
Yeah. Felt good.

But the guitar in the back starts playing by itself — soft, haunting, almost mocking. Desmond glances in the rearview. Golden wings slowly unfold, wrapping the car tighter.

He doesn’t tell Layla.

DESMOND

(whisper)
We’re running… right?

ACT SIX

EXT. CALIFORNIA STATE LINE - DAWN

The Torino crosses the line into California. Sun rising over the desert like a promise.

Desmond and Layla whoop as they pass the sign. Pure joy for a moment.

LAYLA

We did it. We crossed the line.

DESMOND

(grinning wide)
This is just the beginning, baby. Hollywood doesn’t know what’s about to hit it.

But as they crest a hill, the city skyline glimmers in the distance. In the rearview mirror the golden wings are massive now — fully unfolded, almost embracing the car like they’ll never let go.

Desmond’s smile dies. He stares at the mirror in silence.

The guitar hums, satisfied.

SMASH TO BLACK.

END OF EPISODE 2

END CREDITS roll over the full, raw, road-worn version of “Born to Run” by Bruce Springsteen — the anthem they’ve been chasing the whole episode. Golden wing silhouettes pulse behind the text.
