DECADE

Episode 4: “Tiny Dancer”

Story by Adam Carr & Greg Miller • Written by Greg Miller

TELEPLAY • v1.0

TEASER

EXT. HOLLYWOOD HILLS HOUSE PARTY BALCONY - NIGHT

The city sprawls below like a glittering sea. Desmond and Layla slow-dance barefoot on the cool stone, the party a distant hum behind them.

DESMOND

(soft, almost reverent)
This is it. We made it.
You’re gonna marry a music man.

LAYLA

(smiling against his chest)
Hold me closer.

They kiss — deep, slow, full of belief that every mile, every sacrifice, every choice was worth it.

SMASH TO BLACK.

END TEASER

ACT ONE

INT. PARTY - LATER

The night is pure euphoria. Desmond and Layla dance in the middle of the crowd like they own Hollywood. Champagne, laughter, bodies moving.

In a back room jam session, a familiar face sits at an upright piano — the Piano Man. Desmond plugs in. They fall into a loose, joyful groove. Layla stands right beside the piano, singing along with the words she knows, humming the parts she doesn’t, eyes locked on Desmond.

DESMOND

(grinning at her mid-song)
That’s my girl.

A slick, charming A&R GUY (mid-30s, expensive smile, something unnerving behind the charm) watches them from the edge of the room. He claps slowly when the song ends, eyes lingering on Layla a beat too long.

ACT TWO

INT. MOTEL ROOM - SAME NIGHT (LATER)

They stumble back to the cheap motel, still buzzing. Clothes come off in a rush. They make love — passionate, sweaty, tender, full of hope.

Afterward they lie tangled in the sheets.

DESMOND

(quiet, content)
We’re really here. No more running. No more doubt.

LAYLA

(soft, tracing his chest)
Just you and me.

They fall asleep wrapped around each other — the last pure, unbroken moment of “us.”

ACT THREE

INT. RECORDING STUDIO - NEXT DAY

Desmond brings Layla with him to a quick studio session. The same A&R GUY from the party is there, along with engineers and session players. He greets Desmond warmly, but his eyes flick to Layla with calculated interest.

Desmond plays. He’s on fire. The room reacts like they’ve just heard the future. But the focus slowly shifts. People talk over Layla. The A&R Guy barely acknowledges her presence except to offer her a drink with a too-familiar smile.

During a break, while Desmond is pulled into conversation with the engineers, the A&R Guy approaches Layla alone.

A&R GUY

(quiet, charming, with an edge)
Rough watching him get pulled in a hundred directions, huh? First time in the big leagues can feel pretty lonely.

He discreetly offers her a small vial.

A&R GUY (cont’d)

Just a little something to take the edge off. Help you keep up with him. Would be a shame if you got left behind.

Layla hesitates… then pockets it.

ACT FOUR

INT. MOTEL ROOM - EVENING

Desmond returns buzzing with excitement.

DESMOND

They loved it. They’re talking about a showcase next week. Real money, Layla. This is everything we dreamed about.

He tries to pull her close. She pulls back slightly.

LAYLA

(quiet)
You barely looked at me in there. I felt like I was in the way.

DESMOND

(trying to brush it off)
I was working the room. For us.

LAYLA

(soft, heartbroken)
It didn’t feel like “us” today.

ACT FIVE

INT. MOTEL ROOM - LATE NIGHT

Desmond is asleep. Layla sits on the edge of the bed, wide awake.

She looks at him — the man she loves, already slipping away into the machine of fame.

In the mirror across the room, golden wings slowly unfold behind Desmond’s sleeping form. Layla sees them clearly. She doesn’t wake him.

She reaches for the vial, stares at it for a long beat… then puts it away for now.

LAYLA

(whisper, barely audible)
Hold me closer…

The guitar in the corner hums contentedly.

ACT SIX

EXT. SUNSET STRIP - DAWN

Desmond and Layla walk the empty boulevard. He’s still talking excitedly about the future. She forces a smile, but the distance between them is already growing.

DESMOND

This is our town now.

But as they pass a storefront window, golden wings flicker behind Desmond’s reflection. Layla sees them. He doesn’t.

SMASH TO BLACK.

END OF EPISODE 4

END CREDITS roll over a haunting, stripped-down piano-and-strings version of “Tiny Dancer” by Elton John — fragile, beautiful, and bittersweet. Golden wing silhouettes drift across the screen.
