DECADE

A Life Told Through Rock & Roll

CHARACTER BIBLE — SEASON 1 • v1.0

CHARACTER BIBLE

DESMOND “DES” KANE

18 → mid-40s

Sharp cheekbones, barely-leashed hunger, shaggy hair that gets wilder as the story progresses. Charismatic everyman with a poet’s heart and a devil’s appetite.

Core Want: To be seen and worshipped as the rock god he has always felt burning inside him.

Flaw: A hunger so deep it devours everything — and everyone — he loves.

Voice: Swings between fast, rhythmic, passionate rants (Sorkin energy when the music hits) and haunted, deathly silence when the golden wings close in.

Arc: Naïve dreamer → supernova → compromised deal-maker → vengeful husk → man who realizes the guitar owns him.

LAYLA

18 (Season 1)

Dark hair, fierce eyes that already see the ending. Beautiful, ambitious, and dangerous in her idealism.

Core Want: Escape the Midwest trap and become legendary together with Desmond.

Flaw: Romantic idealism blinds her to the red flags — she is the one who first whispers “you’d give anything.”

Arc: Fierce partner and muse → tragic casualty of the deal → ghost that haunts every chord Desmond plays.

THE MAN / THE DEVIL

Appears late 50s (shape-shifter)

Warm smile, ancient eyes, coat always too expensive for the setting. Chameleon performer (one actor or multiple).

Core Want: Souls that choose their own damnation.

Role: Never cartoon evil — always seductive, folksy, then corporate-slick. Frames every offer as “your choice.”

Golden wings motif appears in reflections when he is near.

SUPPORTING CHARACTERS

KEITH — 17, stocky, perpetual baby face, sweat-soaked t-shirt, loyal as a dog. Comic relief → moral compass → early casualty.
TAYLOR — 18, lanky, sharp features, half-assed scarf, already trying to look like a star. Ambitious, sells out first.
MOLLY — Femme fatale. Uses sex and lies as currency. Another of The Man’s plants. Pulls Desmond into the Farrell murder subplot.
AQUALUNG — Grizzled, dying, wealthy ex-military pervert. The rotting mirror of what Desmond is becoming.
