DECADE

Pilot Episode: “Spirit in the Sky”

Story by Adam Carr & Greg Miller • Written by Adam Carr & Greg Miller

PILOT TELEPLAY • v1.0

TEASER

EXT. MIDWEST CORNFIELD - DUSK (1974)

Corn stalks whip past like green knives. DESMOND KANE, 18, all sharp bones and barely-leashed hunger, crashes through them, guitar case slamming his hip.

He bursts into a clearing. A battered Ford Torino idles. LAYLA, 18, dark hair, eyes like she already knows how this ends, leans against the hood smoking.

DESMOND

(breathless, feral grin)
They’re waiting. Whole fucking town.

LAYLA

Then let’s burn it down.

CUT TO:

EXT. SUBURBAN GARAGE - NIGHT

String lights. A dozen locals in lawn chairs, half-drunk, half-bored. Desmond’s band is dying on stage.

KEITH — 17, stocky, perpetual baby face, sweat-soaked t-shirt, loyal as a dog — flails behind the kit.

TAYLOR — 18, lanky, sharp features, already trying to look like a rock star with a half-assed scarf — looks ready to puke.

Desmond sings like he’s clawing at heaven. Voice cracks. Guitar sounds like shit.

The last chord dies. Polite, pitying claps. Someone mutters, “Jesus Christ.”

DESMOND

(into the mic, low and raw)
Thanks for pretending.

He unplugs.

END TEASER

ACT ONE

INT. DESMOND’S BEDROOM - LATER THAT NIGHT

Ceiling and walls plastered with gods: Houses of the Holy, Zeppelin, Floyd, Sabbath, Kiss. The room stinks of weed, sweat, and want.

Desmond and Layla on the narrow bed, passing a joint. Smoke drifts up like a dying prayer.

DESMOND

(quiet, then the engine catches — fast, rhythmic, building)
How the fuck do you want something this bad your whole life and still suck this hard? Not “needs practice.” Not “almost there.” Full-on, embarrassing, laugh-at-you-in-the-parking-lot suck. Since I was four years old, man — four — sitting on that linoleum floor while my old man played Chuck Berry before he shipped to Vietnam… I didn’t want to play guitar. I wanted to be the goddamn lightning. The riff that makes kids ten years from now feel what I felt the first time “Stairway” hit. I want to burn.

LAYLA

(soft, pushing, hungry)
Then burn.

DESMOND

With what? This shithole town? My uncle’s hand-me-down piece of crap that sounds like a dying cat? We’ve got no money, no van, no fucking plan except point the Torino west and hope we don’t rot in Nebraska. That’s not a plan, Layla. That’s suicide with extra steps.

LAYLA

(leaning in, eyes locked on his, voice low and sharp — half challenge, half seduction)
Then do it anyway. You’d give anything, right?

She lets the words sit. Her fingers trace his jaw. The air thickens.

DESMOND

(beat, voice dropping, almost a vow)
Anything.

They kiss — desperate, teeth and need, like they’re sealing something neither fully understands. But as they break, Desmond glances at the Houses of the Holy poster. One of the golden children now has wings — massive, shimmering, beating once, slow and deliberate.

Desmond pulls back, blinking hard. The image is normal again.

DESMOND

(quiet, unsettled)
…Fuck. I’m losing it.

LAYLA

(soft, certain, almost proud)
No. You’re finally waking up.

ACT TWO

EXT. NICK’S PAWN SHOP - NEXT MORNING

Faded sign. Bars on the windows. Desmond walks in carrying his own coffin.

INT. NICK’S PAWN SHOP - CONTINUOUS

Dust and dead dreams. Rifles, typewriters, faded wedding photos.

THE MAN

Desmond Kane. Right on time.

DESMOND

How the hell do you know my name?

THE MAN

Small town. Big hunger. Word travels.

Desmond opens the case. The cheap guitar looks pathetic.

THE MAN

Twenty bucks scrap. But you didn’t come for scrap. You came because the music’s eating you alive. Or you’re killing it. Which is it?

DESMOND

(quiet, then the fire)
Both. Every night I close my eyes and I hear the real thing — not this garage bullshit. The kind that hits like a drug. I want that. I want to burn so bright they write new fucking words for it. I’d give anything—

THE MAN

(cutting in, gentle but steel underneath)
Careful throwing “anything” around, kid.

He reaches under the counter. Lifts out the guitar. Dark wood. Golden filigree that shifts like living feathers. It hums.

THE MAN (cont’d)

This one doesn’t cost money. Costs choice. Play it… the world rips open. Stop playing… it closes forever. Your call. Always has been.

DESMOND

(voice small)
What the fuck are you?

THE MAN

A friend. Or the devil. Depends on the night.

Desmond’s fingers touch the strings. The HUM surges into his bones like lightning and sex at the same time.

DESMOND

(whisper)
…Fuck it.

ACT THREE

EXT. ALLEY BEHIND PAWN SHOP - MOMENTS LATER

Desmond on a milk crate. Tests the guitar.

At first — ordinary. Then the dam breaks. Fingers fly. Riffs pour out vicious and perfect. Notes hang in the air like sparks.

In a puddle, his reflection sprouts golden wings stretching toward the sky. He doesn’t see them. But the music does.

DESMOND

(breathless, raw, almost angry with joy)
Holy shit.

ACT FOUR

INT. GARAGE - EVENING

Band practice. Desmond plugs in. One chord and the air changes. Keith drops a stick. Taylor stares. Layla’s breath catches.

They jam — raw, original, alive. Desmond’s voice is gravel and velvet.

DESMOND

(playing, fast and feral)
You feel that? That’s not garage band shit anymore. That’s us. That’s the fucking ticket out.

LAYLA

(awed, a little scared)
Des… where the hell did you get that guitar?

DESMOND

(grinning like a man who just sold tomorrow)
Made a choice.

In his head, faint echoes only we hear: “Prepare yourself, you know it’s a must…”

ACT FIVE

EXT. COUNTY FAIRGROUNDS - BATTLE OF THE BANDS - NIGHT

Neon. Sweat. Beer. Raw energy.

The Sultans of Swing finish their slick set. Their leader smirks.

Desmond takes the stage. No intro. Just the first devastating riff.

The crowd is slammed. People surge forward. Girls scream. A cop stops writing a ticket to watch.

Desmond solos like the guitar is fucking him back. Golden sparks swirl (we see them). In the wings, The Man claps slowly. Wings flare behind him.

Song ends. Explosion.

CROWD

(chanting)
DES-MOND! DES-MOND!

BACKSTAGE

A&R guy corners them.

A&R GUY

Plane tickets. Whisky showcase. Labels are already calling. You just became dangerous.

DESMOND

(to Layla, quiet fire)
California.

LAYLA

California.

ACT SIX

EXT. OPEN ROAD - NIGHT

The Torino barrels west. Headlights cut the dark. Desmond drives. Layla shotgun, bare feet on the dash. Guitar in the back, humming.

DESMOND

(soft, almost reverent)
We did it. We actually fucking did it.

LAYLA

Told you. You’d give anything.

Desmond smiles — then it dies. The guitar hums louder. In the rearview mirror, golden wings slowly unfold behind both of them, wrapping the car like a closing cage. The hum turns darker, possessive.

Desmond’s hands tighten on the wheel. He says nothing. Just stares, silent, the fire gone. Only dread remains.

DESMOND

(quiet, broken)
…Spirit in the sky.

The wings fill the mirror completely.

SMASH TO BLACK.

END OF PILOT

END CREDITS roll over a brooding, slowed-down, gospel-distorted reimagining of “Spirit in the Sky” — choir bleeding into raw electric howl, like heaven just realized the deal went bad. Golden wing motif pulses behind the text.
