DECADE

Episode 3: “Hollywood Nights”

Story by Adam Carr & Greg Miller • Written by Greg Miller

TELEPLAY • v1.0

TEASER

EXT. HOLLYWOOD BOULEVARD - LATE AFTERNOON (1974)

The Torino crawls through the legendary chaos — palm trees, flashing neon, tourists, runaways, and dreamers. The Hollywood sign looms in the distance like a promise and a warning.

Desmond stares out the window, eyes wide with hunger. Layla leans forward, grinning like she’s already famous.

DESMOND

(voice low, building fast)
Look at this place. They don’t even know we’re here yet. But they will. Every club, every stage, every fucking record exec is about to meet the guy who just got handed the keys to the kingdom.

LAYLA

(grinning, fierce)
Then let’s make them pay attention.

The guitar in the back hums — excited.

SMASH TO BLACK.

END TEASER

ACT ONE

EXT. SUNSET STRIP MOTEL - EVENING

Cheap neon flickers. The Torino is parked crooked. Desmond and Layla haul their bags into a dingy room with a vibrating bed and a cracked view of the Strip.

DESMOND

(pacing, electric)
This is it. No more diners. No more truck stops. Tomorrow I find an open mic or a club that’ll let us play. One set. That’s all I need. One set and they’ll be begging for more.

LAYLA

(throwing her bag on the bed)
We’ve got forty bucks left. Enough for one meal and gas to get to the Whisky if we’re lucky. So make it count.

She pulls him close, kisses him hard — equal parts lust and desperation.

LAYLA (cont’d)

(against his lips)
Burn, baby.

ACT TWO

INT. SMALL CLUB ON THE STRIP - NIGHT

Grimy open-mic night. Smoke-filled room. Desmond plugs in. The crowd is half-drunk, half-indifferent.

He starts playing. The guitar explodes. The room changes instantly. Heads turn. People stop talking. Layla watches from the side, eyes shining with pride and a flicker of fear.

Desmond’s voice is raw power. He tears through an original that feels like it was written by the gods. The set ends to stunned silence… then thunderous applause.

A slick A&R GUY in a polyester suit slides up after the set.

A&R GUY

Kid, where the hell did you come from? That wasn’t local. That was dangerous. Come by my office tomorrow. We’ll talk real money.

Desmond grins like a wolf.

ACT THREE

INT. A&R OFFICE - NEXT DAY

Cheap Hollywood glamour. Posters of current hits. The A&R Guy offers them drinks.

A&R GUY

We can get you in the studio next week. But first — you need a band. Real players. And you need to lose the girlfriend-on-stage thing. Looks too… small town.

LAYLA

(sharp)
I’m not going anywhere.

DESMOND

(fast, protective)
She stays. We’re a package.

The A&R Guy shrugs, but his eyes say otherwise.

ACT FOUR

EXT. HOLLYWOOD HILLS HOUSE PARTY - NIGHT

Big industry party. Celebrities, coke on mirrors, models, and musicians everywhere.

Desmond is pulled into a back room jam session. The guitar sings. Everyone stops to listen. He’s the new guy everyone suddenly wants to know.

Layla watches from across the room as a predatory record exec chats her up, offering “help” for Desmond.

LAYLA

(icy smile)
We don’t need that kind of help.

But she sees Desmond laughing with the execs, already changing.

ACT FIVE

INT. MOTEL ROOM - LATE NIGHT

Desmond comes back buzzing, pockets full of phone numbers and promises.

DESMOND

(fast, manic)
They loved it. They’re talking showcases. Real labels. This is happening, Layla. This is the Hollywood Nights we dreamed about.

LAYLA

(quiet, watching him)
You’re different already. Hungrier.

Desmond pulls her close, but there’s a new distance.

DESMOND

(whisper)
Good. I need to be.

The guitar on the chair hums softly. In the mirror, golden wings flicker behind Desmond. Layla sees it — and says nothing.

ACT SIX

EXT. SUNSET STRIP - DAWN

Desmond and Layla walk the empty boulevard as the sun comes up. The city feels alive and hungry.

DESMOND

This is our town now.

But as they turn a corner, The Man stands on the sidewalk, smiling warmly. He tips an imaginary hat.

THE MAN

(quiet, only Desmond hears)
Enjoy the nights, kid. They don’t last forever.

Desmond stops cold. Layla doesn’t see him.

The golden wings briefly flare in a store window reflection.

DESMOND

(soft, to himself)
Hollywood Nights…

SMASH TO BLACK.

END OF EPISODE 3

END CREDITS roll over a sultry, reimagined version of “Hollywood Nights” by Bob Seger — turned darker, sexier, more dangerous. Golden wing shadows pulse behind the text.
